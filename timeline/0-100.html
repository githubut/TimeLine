0-100 AD: Ancient Times

The 1st century AD marks the beginning of the Common Era, characterized by the birth of Christianity and the consolidation of Roman power across the Mediterranean world.

Major Events

Birth of Jesus Christ (0-1 AD)

Jesus Christ is born in Bethlehem. His teachings would eventually create Christianity, one of the world's major religions affecting billions of people.

Augustus Rules Rome (0-14 AD)

Augustus becomes the first Roman Emperor, establishing the Roman Principate. He brings peace and prosperity to the empire after decades of civil war.

Han Dynasty Flourishes (0-50 AD)

The Han Dynasty in China reaches its golden age with advances in philosophy, literature, and technology. The Silk Road begins facilitating trade with the West.

Crucifixion of Jesus (33 AD)

Jesus is crucified in Jerusalem, becoming the central event in Christian faith. His disciples begin spreading his message throughout the Roman Empire.

Eruption of Mount Vesuvius (79 AD)

Mount Vesuvius erupts in Italy, destroying the cities of Pompeii and Herculaneum. The ash preserves these Roman cities, providing an invaluable window into ancient Roman life.

Siege of Jerusalem (70 AD)

Roman General Titus besieges and destroys Jerusalem, ending the Jewish Revolt. The Second Temple is destroyed, fundamentally changing Jewish history.

Colosseum Construction Begins (72 AD)

Emperor Vespasian commences construction of the Colosseum in Rome. When completed, it becomes one of the greatest engineering achievements of the ancient world.

Trajan Expands Rome (98-117 AD)

Under Emperor Trajan, the Roman Empire reaches its maximum territorial extent. Rome controls territory from Britain to Mesopotamia.

Cultural Developments

Roman Architecture Advances

Romans perfect the use of concrete and arches, enabling the construction of aqueducts, roads, and monumental buildings that still stand today.

Spread of Christianity

Early Christian missionaries, including Paul, travel throughout the Mediterranean spreading Jesus's teachings, establishing the foundation for the religion's global expansion.

Roman Literature Flourishes

Poets like Ovid and historians like Livy produce works that become cornerstones of Western literature and historical knowledge.
