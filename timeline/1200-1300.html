1200-1300 AD: Late Medieval Period

This century sees the development of representative institutions, expanded trade routes, and the late medieval period reaching maturity.

Major Events

Magna Carta Signed (1215 AD)

English King John is forced to sign the Magna Carta, limiting royal power and establishing the principle that no one is above the law.

Fourth Crusade (1202-1204 AD)

Crusaders sack Constantinople itself, establishing a Latin Empire and demonstrating the corruption and complexity of the Crusades.

Parliament Emerges in England (1265 AD)

Simon de Montfort summons the first assembly including common people, establishing the foundation for the modern British Parliament.

Mongol Empire at Peak (1206-1368 AD)

Genghis Khan and his successors build the largest land empire in history, connecting East and West through the Silk Road.

Marco Polo's Journey (1271-1295 AD)

Venetian merchant Marco Polo travels to the court of Kublai Khan, bringing knowledge of distant lands to Europe.

Kublai Khan Establishes Yuan Dynasty (1271 AD)

Kublai Khan founds the Yuan Dynasty in China, uniting the empire after the Mongol conquest.

Cultural Developments

Dante Writes Divine Comedy (1308-1320 AD)

Italian poet Dante Alighieri composes his masterpiece, one of the greatest works of world literature.

Gothic Cathedrals at Peak

Great Gothic cathedrals like Chartres and Notre-Dame represent the zenith of medieval architectural achievement.
