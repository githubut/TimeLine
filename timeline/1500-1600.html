1500-1600 AD: Age of Exploration

The 16th century is defined by European exploration of the world, the Protestant Reformation, and the beginning of European global dominance.

Major Events

Protestant Reformation (1517 AD)

Martin Luther challenges the Catholic Church with his 95 Theses, sparking a movement that divides Christianity and reshapes Europe.

Conquest of Mexico (1519-1521 AD)

Spanish conquistador Hernán Cortés conquers the Aztec Empire. This conquest is brutal but begins European dominance of the Americas.

Francisco Pizarro Conquers Peru (1532-1572 AD)

Spanish conquistador Pizarro defeats the Inca Empire and claims Peru for Spain, extending European control in South America.

Magellan's Voyage (1519-1522 AD)

Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan leads the first expedition to circumnavigate the globe, proving the world's true size.

English Defeat Spanish Armada (1588 AD)

The English navy defeats the Spanish Armada, establishing England as a major naval power and beginning Spanish decline.

Renaissance in Northern Europe

The Renaissance spreads beyond Italy to France, Germany, and other regions, spreading humanist ideas throughout Europe.

Cultural & Religious Developments

Counter-Reformation Begins

The Catholic Church responds to Protestantism with the Counter-Reformation, reforming itself and spreading Catholicism to the Americas.

Shakespeare Writes (1590-1613 AD)

William Shakespeare produces his greatest plays and poetry, becoming one of history's greatest writers.
