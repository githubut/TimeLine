1800-1900 AD: Industrial Age

The 19th century witnesses the full flowering of the Industrial Revolution, imperialism, and the emergence of modern mass society.

Major Events

Napoleonic Wars (1803-1815 AD)

Napoleon conquers much of Europe before being defeated at Waterloo, ending his empire and reshaping European boundaries.

American Civil War (1861-1865 AD)

Devastating war between North and South over slavery. Union victory preserves the United States and ends slavery.

Darwin's Evolution (1859 AD)

Charles Darwin publishes "Origin of Species," revolutionizing biology and challenging religious understanding of human origins.

Edison's Light Bulb (1879 AD)

Thomas Edison perfects the practical electric light bulb, transforming society and enabling night activities.

Industrial Revolution Expands

Steam power, railroads, steel production, and factory systems spread throughout Europe and America, transforming society.

Age of Imperialism

European powers colonize much of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific, creating a global empire.

Cultural & Scientific Developments

Marxist Philosophy Emerges

Karl Marx develops theories of communism and historical materialism, profoundly influencing 20th-century politics.

Romantic and Realistic Art

Art movements emphasize emotion, nature, and later realistic depictions of modern industrial society.
