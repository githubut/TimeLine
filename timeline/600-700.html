600-700 AD: Rise of Islam

This transformative century sees the birth and rapid spread of Islam, which becomes one of the world's major religions and reshapes the Middle East, North Africa, and beyond.

Major Events

Birth of Prophet Muhammad (570-632 AD)

Muhammad is born in Mecca. Around 610 AD, he begins receiving revelations that form the basis of Islam, one of the world's major religions.

Hijra to Medina (622 AD)

Muhammad and followers migrate to Medina, where Islamic community becomes established. This event marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar.

Islamic Expansion Begins (632-661 AD)

After Muhammad's death, the Rashidun Caliphate rapidly expands Islam across Arabia, Syria, Palestine, Egypt, and Persia in just 20 years.

Battle of Yarmouk (636 AD)

Muslim forces decisively defeat the Byzantine Army, securing Islamic control of the Levant and beginning centuries of Islamic rule in the region.

Umayyad Caliphate Established (661 AD)

The Umayyad Dynasty begins, establishing the first hereditary Islamic empire that will rule for nearly a century.

Anglo-Saxon Christianity (600s AD)

Christian missionaries convert Anglo-Saxon kingdoms in Britain. The Synod of Whitby (664 AD) aligns Britain with Rome rather than Celtic Christianity.

Religious & Cultural Developments

Quran Organized into Standard Text

Under Caliph Uthman, the Quran is compiled into a standardized written text, ensuring consistency for Islamic teachings worldwide.

Islamic Science Begins

Muslim scholars begin translating Greek and Persian scientific and philosophical works, laying the foundation for Islamic golden age of learning.
