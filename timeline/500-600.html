500-600 AD: Early Medieval Period

This century sees the consolidation of Germanic kingdoms in Europe, the rise of monasticism, and the birth of a new world religion: Islam.

Major Events

Justinian's Reign (527-565 AD)

Byzantine Emperor Justinian attempts to restore the Eastern Empire, reconquering North Africa, Italy, and Spain from barbarian rulers.

Hagia Sophia Built (532-537 AD)

Justinian commissions the construction of the Hagia Sophia in Constantinople. This architectural marvel remains one of the greatest achievements of human engineering.

Justinian's Code (529 AD)

Emperor Justinian compiles and reforms Roman law into a comprehensive legal code that influences law throughout the world.

Frankish Kingdom Rising

The Frankish kingdom in what is now France grows in power under the Merovingian and then Carolingian dynasties, eventually becoming the dominant power in Europe.

Arthur of Britain (5th-6th century)

Historical or legendary figure of King Arthur represents Romano-British resistance against Saxon invaders in Britain.

Pope Gregory the Great (590-604 AD)

Pope Gregory I strengthens papal authority, reforms Church practices, and sends missionaries to convert pagan kingdoms, including England.

Religious & Cultural Developments

Monastic Movement Expands

Benedict of Nursia creates the Benedictine Rule, establishing a systematic form of monasticism that preserves knowledge and spreads throughout Europe.

Buddhism Spreads in Asia

Buddhism continues to spread throughout Asia, becoming the spiritual foundation for millions while Hinduism remains strong in India.
