300-400 AD: Christianity Becomes Dominant

This century marks a turning point as Christianity transforms from a persecuted minority religion to the official religion of the Roman Empire.

Major Events

Constantine's Conversion (312 AD)

Emperor Constantine I converts to Christianity, fundamentally changing the relationship between the Church and state. Christianity becomes favored religion.

Council of Nicaea (325 AD)

Constantine convenes the first ecumenical council to standardize Christian doctrine and address the Arian controversy. This establishes the Nicene Creed.

Christianity Made Official (380 AD)

Emperor Theodosius I declares Christianity the official state religion of the Roman Empire, ending pagan traditions and establishing Christian supremacy.

Constantinople Founded (330 AD)

Constantine establishes Constantinople as the new capital of the Empire, signaling a shift of power to the East and ensuring the Eastern Empire's survival.

Fall of Rome Begins (378 AD)

The Eastern Roman Empire suffers a catastrophic defeat at the Battle of Adrianople to Gothic forces, beginning the era of barbarian invasions.

Jerome Translates Bible (380s AD)

Saint Jerome completes the translation of the Bible into Latin, creating the Vulgate, which becomes the standard Christian text for centuries.

Religious & Cultural Developments

Christian Church Consolidates Power

The Church becomes wealthy and politically powerful, establishing a hierarchy with the Bishop of Rome gaining prominence.

Monasticism Develops

Monastic communities grow throughout the empire as Christians withdraw from society to pursue spiritual perfection and preserve learning.
