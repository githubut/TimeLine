1700-1800 AD: Age of Enlightenment

The 18th century is defined by the Age of Enlightenment emphasizing reason and individual rights, leading to widespread revolutions.

Major Events

American Revolution (1775-1783 AD)

Thirteen British colonies declare independence and establish the United States of America. They pioneer a democratic republic based on Enlightenment principles.

French Revolution (1789-1799 AD)

Revolutionaries overthrow French monarchy and establish a republic. The revolution spreads ideals of liberty, equality, and fraternity across Europe.

Napoleon's Rise (1799 AD)

Napoleon Bonaparte seizes power in France, becoming one of history's most influential military leaders and administrators.

Industrial Revolution Begins (1760s AD)

Mechanization, steam power, and factory systems begin transforming manufacturing and society, beginning in Britain.

Declaration of Independence (1776 AD)

The thirteen colonies declare independence with a document asserting universal human rights and consent of the governed.

Constitution of the United States (1787 AD)

The U.S. Constitution establishes a federal system with separation of powers, becoming a model for democracies worldwide.

Cultural & Intellectual Developments

Enlightenment Philosophy

Philosophers like Voltaire, Rousseau, and Kant promote reason, individual rights, and democratic government.

Neoclassical Art and Architecture

Art and architecture return to classical simplicity and order after the ornate Baroque period.
