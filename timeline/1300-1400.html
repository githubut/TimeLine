1300-1400 AD: Crisis and Recovery

The 14th century is marked by catastrophic plague, political turmoil, and the gradual emergence of early Renaissance culture.

Major Events

Black Death Pandemic (1347-1351 AD)

Bubonic plague kills 75-200 million people across Europe, Asia, and Africa. Entire populations are decimated, reshaping society forever.

Peasants' Revolt (1381 AD)

English peasants revolt against taxes and feudal obligations, demonstrating growing tensions between social classes.

Fall of Acre (1291 AD)

With the loss of Acre, the last major crusader stronghold falls, ending the Crusading era in the Holy Land.

Hundred Years' War (1337-1453 AD)

England and France engage in a prolonged conflict that devastates both nations and shapes European politics.

Joan of Arc (1412-1431 AD)

The French peasant girl leads military victories, turning the tide in the Hundred Years' War before being captured and executed.

Ming Dynasty Established (1368 AD)

The Ming Dynasty is founded in China after overthrowing Mongol rule, beginning another great period of Chinese civilization.

Cultural Developments

Early Renaissance in Italy

Italian artists and thinkers like Petrarch and Giotto pioneer the Renaissance, reviving interest in classical learning and art.

Chaucer's Work (1387 AD)

Geoffrey Chaucer completes Canterbury Tales, one of the first great works of literature in English.
