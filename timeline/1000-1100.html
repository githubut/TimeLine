1000-1100 AD: Medieval Zenith

The 11th century witnesses the height of medieval feudalism, the beginning of the Crusades, and the continued consolidation of European kingdoms.

Major Events

Norman Conquest of England (1066 AD)

William the Conqueror defeats King Harold at the Battle of Hastings, establishing Norman rule in England and reshaping English culture and language.

First Crusade Launched (1096-1099 AD)

Pope Urban II calls for a crusade to recover Jerusalem from Muslim control. The First Crusade succeeds, establishing Christian kingdoms in the Holy Land.

Great Schism (1054 AD)

The Christian Church splits into Eastern (Orthodox) and Western (Catholic) branches, a division that persists to the present day.

William the Conqueror Becomes King (1066 AD)

After the Battle of Hastings, William becomes King of England, introducing Norman feudalism and dramatically changing English history.

Domesday Book Created (1086 AD)

William commissions the Domesday Book, a comprehensive census of England. It remains one of the most important historical documents.

Solomon's reign in Israel

Solomon establishes himself as king and builds the First Temple in Jerusalem, becoming one of history's legendary wise rulers.

Cultural Developments

Romanesque Architecture Emerges

Churches and castles built in the Romanesque style with rounded arches and sturdy walls dominate medieval architecture.

Medieval Universities Founded

Early universities like Bologna and Oxford establish formal institutions of higher learning, laying groundwork for modern universities.
