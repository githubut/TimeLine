Ancient Era (0-500 AD)

Birth of Jesus Christ

0-1 AD: Christian calendar begins. Major religious figure whose teachings shape Christianity.

Roman Empire Expansion

27 BC - 476 AD: Rome becomes the dominant superpower, controlling vast territories across Europe, Africa, and Asia.

Eruption of Mount Vesuvius

79 AD: Destroys Pompeii and Herculaneum, preserving Roman life in ash for posterity.

Great Wall of China

0-500 AD: Massive fortification system built to protect Chinese dynasties from invasions.

Han Dynasty

206 BC - 220 AD: Golden age of Chinese civilization with advances in philosophy, art, and technology.

Medieval Period (500-1500 AD)

Fall of Rome

476 AD: Western Roman Empire falls, marking the beginning of the Medieval Period in Europe.

Rise of Islam

610 AD: Prophet Muhammad begins preaching Islam, which rapidly spreads across the Middle East and beyond.

Crusades

1095-1291 AD: Series of religious wars between Christians and Muslims for control of Holy Land.

Black Death

1347-1351 AD: Bubonic plague kills 75-200 million people, devastating Europe, Asia, and Africa.

Fall of Constantinople

1453 AD: Ottoman Empire conquers Constantinople, ending Byzantine Empire and the Medieval Age.

Renaissance & Exploration (1400-1600 AD)

Gutenberg Printing Press

1440 AD: Invention revolutionizes information distribution and leads to the spread of knowledge.

Columbus Discovery

1492 AD: Columbus reaches the Americas, opening the age of exploration and European colonization.

Renaissance Art & Science

1400-1600 AD: Artists like Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo create masterpieces; scientific revolution begins.

Reformation

1517 AD: Martin Luther challenges Catholic Church, leading to Protestant Christianity.

Age of Enlightenment & Revolution (1600-1800 AD)

Scientific Revolution

1600-1700 AD: Newton, Galileo, and others revolutionize scientific thinking and methodology.

American Independence

1776 AD: United States declares independence from British rule, establishing democratic governance.

French Revolution

1789-1799 AD: Overthrows monarchy, establishes republic, reshapes European politics and society.

Napoleon's Rise

1799-1815 AD: Napoleon conquers much of Europe, later defeated at Waterloo.

Industrial Revolution & Modern Age (1800-1900 AD)

Industrial Revolution

1760-1840 AD: Mechanization transforms manufacturing, agriculture, and transportation using steam power.

Darwin's Evolution

1859 AD: Charles Darwin publishes "Origin of Species," revolutionizing biological science.

American Civil War

1861-1865 AD: Nation tears apart over slavery; Union victories preserve the United States.

Edison's Light Bulb

1879 AD: Practical electric light transforms society and enables night activities.

20th Century & World Wars (1900-2000 AD)

World War I

1914-1918 AD: First global conflict kills millions; reshapes European empires and borders.

Russian Revolution

1917 AD: Bolsheviks overthrow Tsar, establish Soviet Union, shaping 20th century politics.

World War II

1939-1945 AD: Deadliest conflict in history; Holocaust kills millions; nuclear weapons emerge.

Cold War

1945-1991 AD: Ideological struggle between US and Soviet Union defines global politics for decades.

Moon Landing

1969 AD: Apollo 11 achieves first manned lunar landing; humanity reaches the moon.

Internet Revolution

1990s AD: World Wide Web becomes public; digital revolution begins transforming society.

21st Century & Modern Era (2000-2026 AD)

9/11 Attacks

2001 AD: Terrorist attacks on USA shape global politics, security, and military interventions.

Financial Crisis

2008 AD: Global economic collapse; recession impacts millions worldwide.

Arab Spring

2010-2012 AD: Pro-democracy uprisings sweep Middle East and North Africa.

COVID-19 Pandemic

2019-2026 AD: Global pandemic kills millions; transforms work, education, and public health forever.

Climate Change Awareness

2000s-2026 AD: Growing global recognition of climate crisis; initiatives for renewable energy and sustainability.

Artificial Intelligence Boom

2010s-2026 AD: AI advances rapidly; transforms industries and raises ethical questions about future of work.
