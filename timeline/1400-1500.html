1400-1500 AD: Renaissance & Exploration

The 15th century witnesses the full flowering of the Renaissance in Italy and the beginning of European exploration of the world.

Major Events

Gutenberg Invents Printing Press (1440 AD)

Johannes Gutenberg's movable-type printing press revolutionizes information distribution, enabling mass production of books and accelerating intellectual progress.

Fall of Constantinople (1453 AD)

Ottoman forces conquer Constantinople, ending the Byzantine Empire after 1,100 years and marking the end of the Medieval period.

Columbus Discovers Americ (1492 AD)

Christopher Columbus sails across the Atlantic, reaching the Americas. This opens the age of European exploration and colonization of the New World.

Leonardo da Vinci Flourishes (1452-1519 AD)

Leonardo da Vinci becomes master artist and scientist, embodying Renaissance ideals of combining art, science, and engineering.

Michelangelo Born (1475 AD)

Michelangelo, one of history's greatest artists, is born. He will create the Sistine Chapel ceiling and David.

Vasco da Gama Reaches India (1498 AD)

Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama reaches India by sea, opening direct maritime trade routes to Asia and ending Arab monopolies.

Cultural Developments

Renaissance Reaches Peak in Italy

Artists and thinkers from Brunelleschi to Botticelli create masterpieces that still define high art. Humanism emphasizes human potential and classical learning.

Development of Perspective in Art

Renaissance artists perfect linear perspective, dramatically changing how art represents reality.
