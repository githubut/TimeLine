400-500 AD: The Fall of Rome

The Western Roman Empire collapses under pressure from barbarian invasions, marking the end of classical antiquity and the beginning of the Middle Ages.

Major Events

Visigoth Invasion of Rome (410 AD)

Alaric and the Visigoths sack Rome itself, shocking the ancient world. Though devastating, the city is not destroyed and eventually recovers.

Fall of the Western Roman Empire (476 AD)

The last Western Roman Emperor, Romulus Augustulus, is deposed by Germanic chieftain Odoacer, officially ending the Western Roman Empire.

Vandals Sack Rome (455 AD)

The Vandals capture and plunder Rome, further demonstrating the empire's decline. The Eastern Empire in Constantinople remains strong.

Ostrogothic Kingdom Established (493 AD)

Theodoric the Great, an Ostrogothic leader, establishes a Germanic kingdom in Italy, replacing Roman rule with barbarian governance.

Augustine Dies (430 AD)

Augustine of Hippo, one of Christianity's greatest theologians, dies. His writings profoundly influence Christian thought for centuries.

Eastern Empire Strengthens

While the West falls, the Eastern (Byzantine) Roman Empire remains stable and prosperous under rulers like Theodosius II and Leo I.

Religious & Cultural Developments

Christianity Becomes Refuge for Learning

As secular Roman culture declines, the Church becomes the primary preserver of classical knowledge through monasteries and monks.

Latin Language Evolves

Latin gradually transforms into Romance languages as Germanic peoples settle in Roman territories and linguistic evolution accelerates.
