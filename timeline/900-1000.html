900-1000 AD: Turning Point

As the first millennium draws to a close, Europe remains fragmented under feudalism while kingdoms gradually consolidate and early nations begin to form.

Major Events

Otto I Crowned Emperor (962 AD)

Otto I is crowned Holy Roman Emperor, establishing a Germanic empire that will persist for centuries and dominate Central Europe.

Viking Expansion Peak

Vikings reach the height of their exploration and expansion, establishing settlements in Greenland and North America (Vinland).

Song Dynasty in China (960 AD)

The Song Dynasty is established in China, beginning another period of remarkable intellectual and cultural achievement, including early scientific advancement.

Leif Erikson Reaches North America (1000 AD)

Leif Erikson, son of Erik the Red, sails to North America, making Vikings the first Europeans to reach the New World.

Feudal System Solidifies

Feudalism becomes the dominant system across Europe with complex hierarchies of lords, vassals, and knights governing society.

Millenium Celebrations (1000 AD)

The year 1000 AD marks the end of the first millennium. Many Europeans expect the world to end, but civilization continues and thrives.

Cultural Developments

European Kingdoms Emerge

Nations like England, France, and Germany gradually consolidate power from feudal fragments, beginning the formation of modern nation-states.

Islamic Spain Flourishes

Al-Andalus in Spain becomes a center of learning where Christian, Muslim, and Jewish cultures interact and produce great scientific and philosophical works.
