1900-2000 AD: Modern Era

The 20th century is defined by two world wars, the Cold War, technological revolution, and the emergence of mass media and computers.

Major Events

World War I (1914-1918 AD)

First global conflict kills 20 million. New weapons like tanks and poison gas introduce industrialized warfare.

Russian Revolution (1917 AD)

Bolsheviks overthrow Tsar and establish Soviet Union, creating the first communist state and shaping 20th-century politics.

World War II (1939-1945 AD)

Deadliest conflict in history kills 70-85 million. Holocaust kills 6 million Jews. Nuclear weapons are developed and used.

Cold War (1945-1991 AD)

Ideological struggle between US and Soviet Union defines global politics, dividing world into communist and capitalist blocs.

Moon Landing (1969 AD)

Apollo 11 lands humans on the moon. Neil Armstrong becomes first human to set foot on another celestial body.

Internet Revolution (1990s AD)

World Wide Web becomes public. Computers and the internet begin transforming society, business, and communication.

Cultural & Technological Developments

Mass Media and Education

Television, radio, and film become dominant forms of entertainment and information, shaping culture and politics.

Computer Revolution

From room-sized mainframes to personal computers, computing becomes ubiquitous, enabling unprecedented information processing.

Civil Rights Movements

Movements for racial equality, gender equality, and LGBTQ+ rights challenge discrimination and promote social progress.
