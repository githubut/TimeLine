700-800 AD: Medieval Growth

The Islamic world experiences remarkable intellectual and cultural development while Charlemagne establishes a powerful European empire and revives learning.

Major Events

Battle of Tours (732 AD)

Charles Martel defeats Muslim forces near Tours, France, halting Islamic expansion into Western Europe and preserving Christian dominance in the region.

Charlemagne's Rise (768 AD)

Charlemagne becomes king of the Franks. He would rule for 46 years, vastly expanding his kingdom and becoming one of history's greatest rulers.

Abbasid Caliphate Established (750 AD)

The Abbasid Dynasty overthrows the Umayyads, moving the Islamic capital to Baghdad and establishing an empire focused on learning and culture.

Paper Technology Spreads (750s-800s AD)

Paper-making technology, invented in China, spreads to the Islamic world. This enables easier writing and record-keeping, advancing intellectual progress.

Viking Raids Begin (793 AD)

Viking raiders attack Lindisfarne monastery in England, marking the beginning of Viking expansion that will last for centuries.

Tang Dynasty in China (618-907 AD)

The Tang Dynasty represents the golden age of Chinese civilization with advances in poetry, art, administration, and territorial expansion.

Cultural & Intellectual Developments

Carolingian Renaissance

Charlemagne establishes schools and libraries, promoting literacy and learning. Monks preserve classical texts through careful copying and study.

Islamic Science Flourishes

Baghdad becomes a center of learning where Muslim, Christian, and Jewish scholars collaborate. Mathematics, astronomy, and medicine advance rapidly.
