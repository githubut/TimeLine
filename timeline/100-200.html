100-200 AD: Roman Dominance

This century witnesses the Roman Empire at its height under the Antonine emperors, while Christianity gradually spreads throughout the empire despite occasional persecution.

Major Events

Hadrian's Rule (117-138 AD)

Emperor Hadrian travels the empire strengthening its borders. He commissions the famous Hadrian's Wall in Britain to protect against Pictish invasions.

Antoninus Pius Reigns (138-161 AD)

The Roman Empire experiences a peaceful and prosperous period under Antoninus Pius. This is considered the empire's golden age of stability.

Marcus Aurelius Becomes Emperor (161-180 AD)

The Stoic philosopher Marcus Aurelius becomes emperor. Though facing military challenges, he is remembered as Rome's most philosophical ruler.

Pantheon Rebuilt (126 AD)

The Pantheon is rebuilt in Rome under Hadrian. This architectural marvel with its perfect dome remains one of the best-preserved Roman structures.

Christian Persecution Continues

Various emperors sporadic persecute Christians, including under Marcus Aurelius, yet the faith continues to spread and grow stronger.

Plague of Antoninus (165-180 AD)

A devastating plague spreads throughout the Roman Empire, killing an estimated 5 million people and weakening the military.

Cultural & Religious Developments

Early Christian Communities

Christian communities become more organized with appointed leaders and formal doctrines. The religion attracts followers from all social classes.

Roman Engineering Perfection

Roman aqueducts, roads, and public buildings reach technical perfection. These infrastructure projects remain unsurpassed for centuries.
