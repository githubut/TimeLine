800-900 AD: Medieval Empire

This century sees Charlemagne build a vast European empire, crown himself emperor, and Vikings expand dramatically across Europe and beyond.

Major Events

Charlemagne Crowned Emperor (800 AD)

Pope Leo III crowns Charlemagne Emperor of the Romans on Christmas Day, reviving the Western Roman Empire and establishing papal political power.

Treaty of Verdun (843 AD)

Charlemagne's empire is divided among his grandsons, creating the foundation for modern France, Germany, and Italy.

Viking Settlements Begin

Vikings establish permanent settlements in Ireland, establish routes to Russia and Constantinople, and eventually reach North America.

Feudalism Develops

As central authority weakens after Charlemagne's death, feudalism emerges as the dominant social and political system in Europe.

Islamic Mathematics Advanced

Al-Khwarizmi and other Muslim mathematicians develop algebra and advance number theory, creating the foundations for modern mathematics.

Printing Invented in China (868 AD)

The Diamond Sutra, created in China, is the oldest known printed document, demonstrating early printing technology centuries before Gutenberg.

Cultural Developments

Dark Ages Continue in Europe

Knowledge and learning decline in Western Europe as feudalism replaces centralized authority, though monasteries preserve some classical knowledge.

Islamic Culture Peaks

The Islamic golden age reaches its zenith with incredible advances in science, mathematics, philosophy, and architecture.
