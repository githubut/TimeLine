1100-1200 AD: Crusader Era

The 12th century is dominated by the Crusades, feudalism reaches its peak, and the foundations of the modern world slowly emerge.

Major Events

Second Crusade (1147-1149 AD)

Pope calls for the Second Crusade to reinforce crusader positions in the Holy Land after Muslim victories.

Saladin Defeats Crusaders (1187 AD)

Saladin defeats the Crusaders at the Battle of Hattin and recaptures Jerusalem, prompting the Third Crusade.

Magna Carta Pathway Begins

Conflicts between English kings and nobles begin, eventually leading to the Magna Carta and concepts of limited government.

Anagni Incident (1100s)

Pope-king conflicts demonstrate the tension between religious and secular authority that will shape medieval politics.

Medieval Universities Expand

Paris, Oxford, and other universities become centers of learning. Scholasticism develops as a method of philosophical inquiry.

Troubadours Flourish

Medieval poets and musicians from southern France spread courtly love literature throughout Europe, enriching cultural expression.

Cultural Developments

Gothic Architecture Develops

The revolutionary Gothic style with pointed arches and flying buttresses enables the construction of magnificent cathedrals.

Islamic Learning Transmitted to Europe

Through crusader contact and Spain, Islamic science and philosophy reach Europe, sparking intellectual revival.
