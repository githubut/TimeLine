1600-1700 AD: Scientific Revolution

The 17th century witnesses the Scientific Revolution that fundamentally transforms human understanding of the natural world.

Major Events

Galileo's Observations (1610 AD)

Galileo Galilei uses the telescope to observe celestial phenomena, supporting the heliocentric theory and revolutionizing astronomy.

Newton's Laws (1687 AD)

Isaac Newton publishes his Principia Mathematica, establishing the laws of motion and gravity that explain the physical universe.

Thirty Years' War (1618-1648 AD)

Devastating religious war in Europe kills millions and reshapes the political landscape, establishing nation-state system.

Louis XIV's Reign (1643-1715 AD)

The Sun King makes France the dominant European power. He builds Versailles as the greatest palace in Europe.

Founding of British America (1607 AD)

English colonists establish Jamestown in Virginia, beginning permanent English settlements in North America.

English Civil War (1642-1651 AD)

Civil war between Parliament and royalists reshapes English government. Oliver Cromwell briefly rules as military dictator.

Scientific & Cultural Developments

Scientific Method Established

Scientists like René Descartes develop the scientific method based on observation and mathematics, founding modern science.

Baroque Art and Music Flourish

Baroque style dominates art and music with dramatic, emotional, ornate designs. Composers like Bach create masterpieces.
